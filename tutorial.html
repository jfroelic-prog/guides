StepSnap Tutorial

The whole journey, start to finish. Keep this tab open beside StepSnap while you try each step — use the buttons below or your ← → arrow keys.

🎉 Welcome to StepSnap

StepSnap turns the clicks you make in Chrome into an illustrated, step-by-step guide — then publishes it to our team help site where anyone can read it.

The flow is always the same:

  1. Record your clicks with the extension.
  2. Push the draft to the site.
  3. Edit & publish in the web editor.
  4. Share the guide's link.
💡 This tutorial matches the buttons exactly — when you see 📤 Push draft & edit on site here, that's the real button's name.

1 · Install the extension (once)

  1. Unzip the StepSnap kit somewhere you'll keep it.
  2. In Chrome, open chrome://extensions and switch on Developer mode (top-right).
  3. Click Load unpacked and choose the kit's extension folder.
  4. Click the puzzle-piece 🧩 in Chrome's toolbar and pin StepSnap 📌.

After any update to the extension files, revisit chrome://extensions and press its ↻ reload icon.

2 · Connect publishing (once)

  1. Click the StepSnap icon — the recorder panel opens on the right.
  2. Click the ⚙ gear at the bottom of the panel.
  3. Paste the team key from your setup sheet, then Save.

That's it — the panel can now send guides to the team site.

🔑 The team key is shared by our whole team. Keep it off public documents; your admin can replace it at any time.

3 · Record your clicks

⏹ Stop recording
1 Click the "Sign in" button ✕
2 Click "Settings" ✕
  1. Press ⏺ Start recording in the panel.
  2. Do your task normally, in any tab — every click becomes a step with a screenshot and auto-written text, appearing live in the panel.
  3. Press ⏹ Stop recording when you're done.

Starting again later? Start always begins a clean slate (it asks before clearing leftover steps).

4 · Tidy right in the panel

  • ✕ delete a step you didn't mean to record
  • ↑ ↓ move a step earlier or later
  • ⇣ Import Scribe pull in an old Scribe guide (open it in a tab first)
  • 🔧 Test this tab if clicks ever stop recording, this tells you why
  • 🗑 Clear all throw everything away and start over

Only quick fixes happen here — all the real editing tools live on the web, next.

5 · Push the draft to the site

  1. Click 📤 Push draft & edit on site.
  2. Name the guide — this becomes its title and its web address.
  3. Click 📤 Push it. The web Admin opens in a new tab, and the panel clears itself (your draft is safe on the site).
🔒 Drafts are private — they never appear on the public homepage until you publish.

6 · Log in to the web Admin

  • First time on a computer: paste the team key and choose a password of your own for that device.
  • Every time after: just your password.

The small “admin build …” line on the login screen tells you which version you're running — handy if something ever looks out of date.

7 · Open your draft

In the Admin, find the 📥 Drafts list — your pushed guide is waiting there.

Click ✏️ Open & edit to load it into the editor.

Already-published guides are edited the same way: find them under 🗂 Guides and click ✏️ Edit.

8 · Write and organise the steps

  • titles & text click any title or description and type
  • ● Step ▾ the dropdown turns a step into a 📖 Chapter heading or a 💡 Tip / ⚠️ Alert / 🔔 Warning note
  • ↑ ↓ / drag reorder steps (grab the number badge to drag)
  • + Step · 📖 Chapter the faint dashed bars between cards insert right there
  • ⧉ duplicate a step  ·  ✕ delete it

Chapters give long guides a floating table of contents, and step numbers restart in each chapter.

9 · Annotate the screenshots

Click Save here
  • 🎯 Add circle a click-here ring (drag it into place)
  • ▭ Draw box drag to outline an area
  • ➤ Arrow drag from the arrow's tail to its tip
  • 🔤 Label a little text sign — type its wording in the tool bar box
  • colour square recolours the selected shape
  • 🌫 Blur drag over anything sensitive — hidden for good when published
  • 📷 replace the screenshot with an image file
  • 🖼✕ remove the screenshot

Drag any shape to move it · the little corner square resizes it (or moves an arrow's tip) · its ✕ deletes it · ↩ Undo fixes mistakes.

10 · Nice extras

  • 🎞 GIF tick a few steps and combine them into a looping animation
  • 📋 Copy guide copies the whole formatted guide — paste into Gmail, Google Docs, or Word
  • ⬇ Markdown downloads the guide as a text file with images

11 · Publish 🚀

  1. Type your name in Made by so the guide shows who wrote it.
  2. Click 🚀 Publish.
  3. The guide is live on the team site about a minute later, and the draft cleans itself up.

Publishing again with the same guide updates the same web address — links you've shared keep working.

12 · Share it

  • 🔗 Copy on the guide's row in the Admin — or on its homepage tile, or on the guide page itself — copies the link to send out
  • ⬇ PDF on every guide page saves a printable copy
  • Google Sites the whole homepage can be embedded: Insert → Embed → By URL → whole page

13 · Manage the site (admins)

  • ↑↓ + ⭐ arrange the homepage order, star the featured row, then Save layout
  • 🙈 Unlist keep a guide's link alive but hide it from the homepage
  • ✎ Rename change a guide's web address  ·  🗑 Remove take it off the site
  • 🩺 Health check scans every live guide for problems

You're ready! 🎉

Wherever you are in StepSnap, the ❓ button shows a quick reminder of what every icon does — and this tutorial is always at …/tutorial.html on the team site.
