StepSnap — Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 22, 2026

StepSnap is a free, self-hosted tool for turning your clicks into step-by-step how-to guides. This policy explains what the StepSnap browser extension does with your data. In short: the extension keeps your data on your own computer and only sends it to a destination you choose and control. There is no StepSnap company server, and nothing is sent to the developer.

The one-sentence version: the extension records screenshots locally in your browser, and the only time it sends anything over the network is when you click “Push draft,” at which point your guide is uploaded to the GitHub repository you set up, using the access key you provided.

What the extension handles

  • Screenshots and step text. While you are recording, the extension captures screenshots of the tab you are recording and writes short descriptions of what you clicked. These are stored locally in your browser (chrome.storage.local) until you delete them or push a draft.
  • Your publishing key. If you set up publishing, the GitHub access token you paste is stored locally in your browser and is used only to communicate with GitHub’s API on your behalf. It is never sent anywhere else.
  • Your name (optional). If you enter a “Made by” name, it is stored locally and included in the guides you publish.

What the extension sends, and where

  • To GitHub — only when you choose to publish. When you push a draft, the guide (its screenshots and text) is uploaded to the GitHub repository you configured, using your own access key. You control that repository and that key.
  • When importing from Scribe. If you use the Scribe importer, the extension reads the Scribe guide open in your active tab and downloads its screenshots so they can be included in your guide.

What the extension does not do

  • It does not send any data to the developer or to any StepSnap server — there is no such server.
  • It does not include analytics, advertising, or tracking of any kind.
  • It does not sell or share your data with third parties.
  • It does not read pages or capture screenshots except while you are actively recording.

Permissions, in plain terms

  • Access to websites you record — so it can capture a screenshot and note your click on whatever page you are documenting.
  • Tabs & scripting — to know which page each step belongs to and to place the click-recorder into pages you are recording.
  • Storage — to hold your captured steps and screenshots on your own computer (guides can be large, so extra storage is requested).
  • Side panel — the recording controls live in the browser side panel.

Your control

Everything the extension stores lives in your browser. You can clear the current steps at any time from the panel, and removing the extension deletes its stored data. Guides you have published live in your own GitHub repository, which you manage directly.

Contact

Questions about this policy can be sent to the StepSnap administrator for your team.
